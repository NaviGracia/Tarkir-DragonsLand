Tarkir, The Dragon's Land

Tarkir, once a war-torn plane defined by clan rivalries and the extinction of dragons, has been irrevocably changed. In the current timeline, dragons rule the skies, and the old power structures have either been reshaped or swept away. Each clan, once led by a khan, now serves—or resists—the will of a mighty Dragonlord. Ancient magic stirs amidst the ruins of what was, and old grudges burn with new fire. Tarkir is a reborn world—vibrant, perilous, and forever altered—where history has been rewritten, but the struggle for dominance endures.

The Abzan Houses

Once defined by endurance and tradition, the Abzan valued family ties and honor above all else. In the dragon-dominated Tarkir, they now serve under Dromoka, whose teachings have reshaped their culture into one that worships unity through strength and obedience. The old ways are fading, but a quiet resistance still simmers beneath the surface.

The Jeskai Way

Seekers of enlightenment and discipline, the Jeskai once blended martial prowess with mystical insight. Under Ojutai, the clan now venerates knowledge as divine truth, treating the dragon as both mentor and god. The contemplative monks have become devoted scholars, though not all agree with the rigid control of their so-called spiritual guide.

The Sultai Brood

Ruled by cunning and greed, the Sultai once embraced dark magic and necromancy to maintain their power. Now, under the merciless gaze of Silumgar, their ambitions remain—but with a constant threat of betrayal or death. Loyalty is fickle, and survival often depends on one's ability to manipulate from the shadows.

The Mardu Horde

A clan of speed, fury, and relentless aggression, the Mardu once lived for battle and the thrill of the charge. Under Kolaghan’s reign, they still do—but now they worship the storm of war incarnate. Swift and brutal, the Mardu have become even more volatile, their freedom traded for blood-soaked zealotry.

The Temur Frontier

Embodying ferocity and connection to nature, the Temur prized instinct and strength. With Atarka as their Dragonlord, primal hunger now dictates their way of life. The clan reveres raw power and survival above all else, living in a brutal harmony with their savage environment and their voracious leader.
